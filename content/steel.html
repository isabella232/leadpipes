Your pipes are probably galvanized steel.

That’s good, but not great. Lead can build up on the inside of rusty pipes, so they can continue to release lead — even if the lead piping it was once connected to has been replaced.

And it’s still possible that you might have some lead fittings or solder in the plumbing in your house. It’s probably a good idea to use a filter that is certified to remove lead (it should say “NSF/ANSI Standard 53” on the package).

If you want to know if lead is likely leaching into your water, request a test from your water utility.
